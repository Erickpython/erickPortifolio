MR. ERICK WAMBUGU

Meteorologist || Teacher Leader ||Weather and Climate Analyst || Web Developer || Founder @ Read Connect

Erick Wambugu is a passionate Meteorologist, Educator, and aspiring Web Developer committed to driving change at the intersection of science, education, and technology.

I hold a First Class Honors degree in Meteorology from South Eastern Kenya University and currently serve as a Teacher Leader in an underserved school Ngwata Primary and Junior School in Mlolongo, Machakos County under the Teach for Kenya fellowship.

I am the founder of Read Connect, a grassroots literacy initiative focused on improving reading outcomes through play-based learning, digital tools, and community engagement. I'm also experienced in weather data analysis and forecasting, having completed an industrial attachment at the Kenya Meteorological Department.

Passionate about tech and its power to solve local challenges, I am actively learning web development with a focus on building solutions that enhance education and climate awareness. My work is deeply inspired by the Sustainable Development Goals especially SDG 4 (Quality Education) and SDG 13 (Climate Action) and I am committed to merging science, education, and digital innovation to create a more informed and resilient society.

WORK EXPERIENCE

  • Founder Read Connect

    March 2024 - upto Date:

    Read Connect is a grassroots literacy initiative I founded in 2024 to bridge the reading gap among learners in underserved communities across Kenya. Our mission is to empower students with essential reading skills through a free, accessible e-library and engaging learning tools. By fostering a culture of reading, Read Connect aims to unlock academic potential and lifelong opportunities—ensuring that every child, no matter their background, has the chance to thrive through literacy.

  • Teach for Kenya Fellowship

    January 2024 - upto Date

    I currently serve as a Fellow with Teach For Kenya, a leadership development program that places driven graduates and professionals in underserved public schools to tackle educational inequity. Through this two-year fellowship, I teach at Ngwata Primary and Junior School in Mlolongo, Machakos County, where I work to improve student outcomes while addressing deeper systemic challenges. Beyond the classroom, the fellowship equips me with training in education leadership, community engagement, and social innovation—empowering me to become a long-term advocate for quality education in Kenya.

  • Industrial Attachment IMTR - KMD

    May 2022 - August 2022

    In 2022, I undertook a professional industrial attachment at the Kenya Meteorological Department’s Institute for Meteorological Training and Research (IMTR), specifically within the Numerical Weather Prediction (NWP) Lab. Over the course of this attachment, I gained hands-on experience in accessing and interpreting satellite imagery, running weather forecasting models such as WRF and GFS, and working with model outputs to produce visual forecasts. I also developed skills in scripting, server navigation (SSH), and using tools like McIDAS for atmospheric data handling. This experience significantly enhanced my technical abilities in operational meteorology, data analysis, and climate forecasting—grounding me further in applied environmental science.
